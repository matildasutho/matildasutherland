Heightened : Make or become more intense
Multisensory : Relating to or involving several physiological senses

Russell's Circumplex Modal of Affect

My earliest memory of a multisensory experience was around the age of 3, at a robotic dinosaur exhibition d at a museum in Sydney. I was with my parents and walking through a dimlit space with moving mechanical dinosaurs either side - lifelike skins and all. All that separated us and them was a rope bollard. At one point my dad reached for the open mouth of a stegosaurus, distressing me so much that I jumped off his shoulders and ran outside crying.
Till around the age of 11 I had two recurring dreams centred around giant, robotic dinosaurs eating members of my family. Clearly this experience affected me, reaching the deepest part of my subconscious and even permeating my dreams. This makes me think of the power we have in creating multisensory works - including the importance of trigger warnings for strobing. As Voltaire stated: with great power comes great responsibility…

The visceral and Feminism?
I’d like to use the blackbox. Perhaps an extension of last semesters project, My Love Language is Digital but reimagined as FELT. I like that visceral is tied to the intuition. Audio/Visual example of visceral (linked by Liam Wolfe during group discussion).
Danny Elfman, Sorry 2021

Initially this resonated with me because I'd like to explore the world of 3D in the digital media course. The animation has a sort of Stranger Things (2016) vibe to it - with the big black monster that plumes like a nuclear bomb over their town in what I think is the last episode of last season. The organ-like shapes are in my view a direct reference to the viscera, and the way they pulsate is reminiscent of peristalsis!

The use of panning of different sonic elements feels visceral. It's quite discombobulating, almost makes you feel ill. Pairing the main vocals with the warped footage of the stylized character singing / close-up of the teeth as they chatter relates it again to the viscera. There's a lot of direct reference to body parts, which I interpret as intention to evoke a guttural sensation.

In the last 45 seconds the word suffocate becomes a motif. Again, being reminded of physicality, using (what I'm assuming is) a metaphor to introduce a bodily response to the narrative.

Ansome, Chocka Block 2019

An example of industrial sounds used to create a visceral experience. The disortion used on the kick drum evokes something guttural. I would classify this as EBM / industrial. EBM stands for Electronic Body Music. Again, this direct reference to the body // viscera... EBM is a combination of industrial / punk music which came about in the 1980s charactierized by 4/4 drum beats and distortion (see: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electronic_body_music).

I like the idea of EBM as a "visceral" genre because its name insinuates this same evocation of guttural/bodily movement. As through you have no choice but to dance. Raises a Q... do we relinquish power of choice when we trust our gut?

Paula Temple, Deathvox 2014

This is a visceral industrial techno track, with an accompanying video. The video uses abrupt cutting to bring it into the visceral realm. The music itself is also very visceral, opting for the highly mechanic and industrial realm of visceral.
